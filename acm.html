The UMass ACM is an officially chartered chapter of the Association of Computing Machinery, the largest network of folks in computing in the world. We fall under the ACM constitution with our own bylaws, providing us with the infrastructure to administer our chapter independently.

We are a CICS body, entrusted with growing and harboring interests and research in everything tech.

Our team has an umbrella structure where our executive board extends 5 different Special Interest Groups, each with their own executive board, members and operations. Currently, we have SIGs for Competitive Programming, Game Developement, Machine Learning, Blockchain and UI/UX Design.


Responsibilites

In my capacity as Vice Chair, I'm responsible for:
  • leading our groups via our roadmap to our vision to grow by organization and incentivization
  • organizing proper marketing and communication pipelines for our groups
  • managing the executive boards of all our Special Interest Groups
  • bringing tech recruiters to our schools for events and talks.
  • creating a culture of collaboration across CICS bodies on campus.

Achievements

Under my leadership, the UMass ACM has:

  • won the global ACM award for Best Recruitment Program for having the most effective recruitment strategies.
    In order to grow our numbers, I set up a dedicated marketing team, managing our events and socials.
  • started the ICPC and Blockchain groups which were previously defunct.
    Our blockchain group is being led by Gavin Andresen, co-founder of Bitcoin.
  • seen greater collaboration across groups due to streamlined and centralized communication channels.
